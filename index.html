Welcome to nature's most amazing light show. This is directly underneath the aurora oval--one of the best places on earth to watch the aurora borealis, the spectacular atmospheric phenomenon better known as the Northern Lights. Late winter and early spring are the best times for the aurora, so be sure to stop in during the months of February and March!

Enjoy with us!

The northern lights, or the aurora borealis, are beautiful dancing waves of light that have captivated people for millennia. But for all its beauty, this spectacular light show is a rather violent event. Energized particles from the sun slam into Earth's upper atmosphere at speeds of up to 45 million mph, but our planet's magnetic field protects us from the onslaught.

Experience with us!

Witnessing the northern lights is a transformational bucket-list experience that many people wait years even a lifetime to see, since the conditions need to be perfect for this spectacular display to take place. In order to increase your chances of seeing this spectacle, you should time your trip to a new moon, and it’s important to be on alert.

Under the Mountains

The northern lights and their counterpart in the southern hemisphere appear when highly charged solar wind particles flowing from the sun collide with air molecules in the earth’s atmosphere transferring their energy into light.
